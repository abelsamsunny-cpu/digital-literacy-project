# [cite_start]Case Study: Phishing Attacks on University Students [cite: 77]

[cite_start]**What the crime is:** Phishing is a type of social engineering where attackers masquerade as a trusted entity to steal sensitive data such as login credentials or financial information. [cite: 78]

**How it typically happens:** In a university setting, a student receives an email that appears to be from the "University IT Desk" or "Library Services." The email claims there is an issue with the student's account and provides a link to "validate credentials." This link leads to a fake login page that looks identical to the official college portal. [cite_start]Once the student enters their username and password, the attacker captures the data. [cite: 78]

[cite_start]**Target Audience:** Students are often targeted because they frequently use various online portals and may be prone to clicking links quickly during busy exam seasons. [cite: 78]

**Consequences:** The attacker gains access to the student’s official email, which can be used to reset passwords for bank accounts, steal personal documents, or send further phishing emails to the entire campus directory using a legitimate account. [cite_start]This can lead to financial loss, identity theft, and severe academic disruption. [cite: 78, 79]
